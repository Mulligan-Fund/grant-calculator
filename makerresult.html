5 Tips for Streamlining Your Process

Was your net grant lower than you expected?

NET GRANT: The grant money available to apply to real work, after subtracting the cost of applying for, monitoring, and reporting on a grant. "Because it took us 40 hours to apply for that $5,000, the net grant was less than $3,000."

Follow these 5 Tips:

  1. Investigate Project Streamline's 4 Core Principles

    • Take a fresh look at your organization's application and reporting requirements
    • Right-size Grant Expectations
    • Relieve the Burden
    • Improve Communication

  2. Discover 5 Practices that Matter

    Find some practical advice for online application systems, financial reporting, implementing staged application processes well, and simplifying requirements.

  3. Access Project Streamline's Champions Toolkit

    Use these practical tools and resources to understand your current practice, make the case for streamlining, benchmark against colleague organizations, and identify opportunities to make meaningful change.

  4. Do Your Homework

    Our report, Drowning in Paperwork, Distracted from Purpose, formed the basis for Project Streamline and continues to be used by grantmakers to better understand inefficiencies in their system. Barriers to change, and principles grantmakers can adopt to improve their practice.

  5. Join a Network

    PEAK Grantmaking is a community for grantmakers who want to do their work more effectively and efficiently. CONNECT with others doing this work every day to share tips, tricks and resources.
